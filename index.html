Regulatory Compliance — Market Requirements

Certificates and registrations required for shipping clear aligners / medical devices to each market.

EUROPE (EU / UK / DACH) ▼
EUROPEAN UNION (27 Member States)
EU MDR 2017/745 — CE Marking via Notified Body (Class IIa medical device)
ISO 13485:2016 — Quality Management System certification
EU Authorized Representative — Required if manufacturer is outside EU
UDI (Unique Device Identification) — Required under EU MDR
EUDAMED Registration — EU database for medical devices
Biocompatibility Testing — ISO 10993 series
UNITED KINGDOM (Post-Brexit)
UKCA / CE Marking — CE marking still accepted (extended recognition)
MHRA Registration — Medicines and Healthcare products Regulatory Agency
UK Responsible Person — Required for non-UK manufacturers
ISO 13485:2016 — Quality Management System
DACH (Germany, Austria, Switzerland)
EU MDR CE Marking — Same as EU (DE, AT are EU members)
Switzerland MRA — Mutual Recognition Agreement with EU (Swissmedic oversight)
UNITED STATES (USA) ▼
FDA 510(k) Clearance — Required. Clear aligners are Class II medical devices. Must demonstrate substantial equivalence to a predicate device.
FDA Establishment Registration — Manufacturing facility must be registered with FDA
ISO 13485:2016 — Quality Management System certification
QMSR (21 CFR Part 820) — FDA Quality Management System Regulation, aligned with ISO 13485 effective Feb 2026
UDI Compliance — Unique Device Identification required per FDA rules
MDR / Adverse Event Reporting — Post-market surveillance obligations
US Agent — Required if manufacturer is outside the US
Biocompatibility Testing — ISO 10993 series
CANADA ▼
Health Canada Medical Device Licence (MDL) — Required. Class II device licence via Health Canada
MDSAP Certificate — Medical Device Single Audit Program (recognized by Health Canada, FDA, TGA, ANVISA, Japan PMDA)
ISO 13485:2016 — Quality Management System
Canadian Medical Device Regulations (SOR/98-282) — Compliance with CMDR
Canadian Authorized Representative — Required if manufacturer is outside Canada
Bilingual Labeling — English + French required
AUSTRALIA ▼
TGA (Therapeutic Goods Administration) Registration — Required. Included in ARTG (Australian Register of Therapeutic Goods)
ISO 13485:2016 — Quality Management System
TGA Conformity Assessment — TGA recognizes EU CE, FDA, Health Canada certifications (expedited pathway)
Australian Sponsor — Local sponsor required for importing medical devices
UDI Requirement — Mandatory from 1 July 2026 (starting with higher-risk classes)
MIDDLE EAST / ISRAEL ▼
SAUDI ARABIA (SFDA)
SFDA MDMA — Medical Device Market Authorization
Local Authorized Representative — Required for overseas manufacturers
Arabic + English Labeling — Mandatory bilingual labeling
Prior FDA/CE approval accelerates review
UAE (MOH)
UAE MOH Registration — Ministry of Health device registration
Free Zone Authority Licence — If operating from DHCC/JAFZA
45-day EDE Review — Fastest in the region
ISRAEL (AMAR / MOH)
AMAR Registration — Israeli Medical Device Registry
MOH Import Licence — Ministry of Health approval
Accepts FDA/CE as reference — Expedited if FDA 510(k) or CE marking exists
ALL MIDDLE EAST MARKETS
ISO 13485:2016 — Universal requirement
Certificate of Free Sale — From country of manufacture
Halal Certification — May be required depending on materials
LATIN AMERICA (LATAM) ▼
BRAZIL (ANVISA)
ANVISA Registration (Registro) — Mandatory for Class II+ devices
BGMP Certificate — Brazilian Good Manufacturing Practice inspection
Local BRH (Brazilian Registration Holder) — Required
Portuguese Labeling — Mandatory
Timeline: 12-24 months
MEXICO (COFEPRIS)
COFEPRIS Registration — Required for all medical devices
Local Responsible Party — Mandatory Mexican representative
Spanish Labeling — Mandatory
Timeline: ~30 business days (decision after complete submission)
ARGENTINA (ANMAT)
ANMAT Registration — Medical Product Registry
Local Authorized Importer — Must be ANMAT-authorized
Spanish Labeling — Mandatory
ALL LATAM
ISO 13485:2016 — Universal
MDSAP — Accepted by Brazil, Canada, USA, Australia, Japan
Certificate of Free Sale
UNIVERSAL REQUIREMENTS (ALL MARKETS) ▼
ISO 13485:2016 — Quality Management System for Medical Devices (required in every market)
ISO 10993 — Biological Evaluation of Medical Devices (biocompatibility testing)
Risk Management (ISO 14971) — Required for all medical device markets
Technical File / Design Dossier — Comprehensive documentation of device design, testing, manufacturing
Post-Market Surveillance (PMS) — Ongoing vigilance and adverse event reporting
MDSAP — Medical Device Single Audit Program (recognized by USA, Canada, Brazil, Australia, Japan)
Certificate of Free Sale — Commonly required by importing countries
Note: Clear aligners are generally classified as Class II medical devices across all major regulatory frameworks. Device classification determines the regulatory pathway and level of scrutiny. Always verify current requirements with regulatory counsel as rules evolve.
